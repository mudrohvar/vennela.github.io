Google Cloud Platform • 2022-Present

Improving Developer Onboarding Experience

Led a cross-functional initiative to redesign the GCP onboarding flow, making it easier for developers to get started with cloud services.

The Challenge

New developers were struggling to get started with GCP, facing three major pain points:

  • Complex Setup: The initial configuration required 15+ steps and often took developers 2-3 hours to complete
  • Unclear Documentation: Critical information was scattered across multiple pages, making it hard to find the right solution
  • Lack of Guidance: First-time users didn't know which services to use for their specific use case

User research revealed that 40% of developers abandoned the onboarding process before completing their first deployment, leading to significant revenue loss and poor developer experience.

Research & Discovery

To deeply understand the problem, I conducted:

  • User Interviews: Spoke with 50+ developers across startups, enterprises, and individual developers
  • Competitive Analysis: Analyzed onboarding flows from AWS, Azure, and other cloud providers
  • Data Analysis: Examined funnel metrics, drop-off points, and support ticket patterns
  • Usability Testing: Watched developers attempt the current onboarding flow and noted pain points

Key insight: Developers didn't want to "learn GCP"—they wanted to solve a specific problem. Our onboarding was product-centric when it should have been use-case-centric.

The Solution

Based on research insights, we implemented three major improvements:

1. Guided Setup Wizard

Created an intelligent wizard that asks developers about their use case and automatically configures the right services. Reduced setup from 15+ steps to just 3-4 decisions.

2. Use-Case Templates

Developed pre-configured templates for common scenarios (web app hosting, data pipeline, ML model deployment) that developers could deploy with one click.

3. Interactive Tutorials

Built in-product tutorials that guide developers through their first deployment with real-time feedback and helpful tips at each step.

Implementation Challenges

The project wasn't without obstacles. Key challenges we overcame:

  1. Technical Complexity: Required coordination across 8 different microservices owned by separate teams
  2. Backward Compatibility: Had to maintain support for existing workflows while rolling out new experience
  3. Localization: Needed to support 15+ languages while maintaining quality of technical content
  4. Performance: Interactive tutorials had to load in under 1 second to maintain developer trust

Results & Impact

After a phased rollout over 6 months, we achieved significant improvements:

  • Onboarding completion rate increased from 60% to 92%
  • Time-to-first-deployment decreased from 2.5 hours to 35 minutes average
  • Support tickets related to onboarding dropped by 67%
  • Net Promoter Score (NPS) for first-time users improved from 32 to 58
  • Feature adoption increased by 45% as users discovered relevant services earlier

The project became a template for other GCP teams and directly contributed to a 25% increase in developer sign-ups quarter-over-quarter.

What I Learned

"This project reinforced that the best product improvements come from deeply understanding user context. Developers didn't need better documentation—they needed guidance that met them where they were. It also taught me the importance of aligning multiple stakeholders around a shared vision, as success required buy-in from 6+ different teams across the organization."

The Problem

Despite Amazon's reputation for great UX, our checkout flow had remained largely unchanged for years. Analysis revealed that 28% of users abandoned their cart during checkout, costing millions in potential revenue.

Main issues identified:

  • Too many steps (5-page flow) created unnecessary friction
  • Mobile experience was particularly poor with tiny tap targets and excessive scrolling
  • Payment method selection was confusing for users with multiple saved cards
  • Delivery options weren't clearly communicated upfront

Research Approach

Conducted comprehensive research including:

  • Analyzed 10M+ checkout sessions to identify drop-off patterns
  • Heat mapping and session recordings to understand user behavior
  • 15 in-person usability studies watching users complete purchases
  • Survey of 5,000+ customers about their checkout experience
  • Competitive benchmarking against Shopify, Walmart, and other e-commerce leaders

The Solution

Redesigned the checkout flow with these key improvements:

Single-Page Checkout

Condensed the 5-page flow into a streamlined single-page experience that showed all information at a glance while maintaining mobile responsiveness.

Smart Defaults

Implemented ML-powered default selections for shipping address and payment method based on purchase history and context.

Progressive Disclosure

Showed only essential information by default with option to expand for details, reducing cognitive load.

A/B Testing Strategy

Given the scale and risk, we took a methodical approach to rollout. Ran 12 different A/B tests over 4 months, starting with small traffic percentages and gradually increasing as we gained confidence.

Key learning: The single biggest win (8% conversion lift) came from simply pre-selecting the user's most frequently used payment method—a small change that had massive impact at Amazon's scale.

Results

  • 20% overall increase in checkout conversion rate
  • 35% reduction in cart abandonment
  • $15M additional revenue per quarter
  • Mobile conversion improved by 28% (vs. 15% on desktop)
  • Checkout completion time reduced from 3.5 minutes to 1.2 minutes average

The Challenge

EY's consulting teams were using 20+ different tools for project management, time tracking, and client deliverables. This fragmentation led to inefficiencies, duplicate data entry, and poor visibility into project status.

The goal: Build a unified platform that would streamline workflows while being flexible enough to support different business units with unique requirements.

Solution & Impact

Led the product strategy and roadmap for a comprehensive platform that integrated project management, time tracking, resource allocation, and client deliverables in one place.

  • Reduced time spent on administrative tasks by 65%
  • Improved project visibility leading to 15% better resource utilization
  • Decreased billing cycle time from 30 days to 7 days average
  • Achieved 92% user adoption rate within 6 months of launch

The platform became the foundation for EY's digital transformation initiative and is still in use today, now serving over 100,000 employees globally.
