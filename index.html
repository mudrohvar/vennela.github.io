How I Think About Products

Start with People

Every great product begins with understanding a real human need. I spend time with users, not just their data, to understand what they actually want to accomplish.

Simplicity is Hard Work

Making something simple requires understanding all the complexity underneath. I believe in doing the hard work of simplification so users don't have to.

Details Matter

The difference between a good product and a great one is often in the small details. I care about the micro-interactions that make experiences feel magical.

"Products are not just features and functions. They're relationships between people and solutions to their problems. My job is to nurture those relationships."
— My approach to product management
